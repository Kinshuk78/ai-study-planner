Course: Foundations of Data Science for Academic Research

Week 1 introduces linear algebra for data analysis, including vectors,
matrices, dot products, matrix multiplication, and the geometric meaning
of projections. Students should be able to represent a small dataset as a
matrix and explain why linear transformations matter for modelling.

Week 2 covers regression modelling. Students learn how ordinary least
squares fits a line by minimising squared residuals, how coefficients are
interpreted, and why assumptions such as linearity, independence, and
constant variance affect academic research claims.

Assessment: students complete a short quiz after each topic, submit a
mini analysis of a study-time dataset, and explain how the model supports
or limits a research conclusion.
