Regression is a statistical method for estimating how an outcome changes
with one or more predictors. In an academic study-planning example, a
researcher might model final exam score as a function of weekly study
hours, attendance, and prior quiz performance.

Ordinary least squares chooses coefficients that minimise the sum of
squared residuals. A residual is the difference between an observed
outcome and the model prediction. Smaller residuals mean the fitted line
is closer to the observed data, although a close fit does not prove a
causal relationship.

Regression results should be interpreted with assumptions in mind.
Linearity means the expected change in the outcome is constant for each
unit change in a predictor. Independence means one student's error should
not determine another student's error. Constant variance means prediction
errors should have similar spread across the range of fitted values.
